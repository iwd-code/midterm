Typography Timeline

  • 1500 BC
  • 1400 AD
  • 1850 AD
  • 1930 AD

Before & After 1500 BC
* * *

25,000 BC - Cave paintings/Pictographs

Beginnings of written language: cave paintings are created and pictographs (Symbols used to represent objects in nature) evolved.

3100 BC - Cuneiform

Sumerians created Cuneiform, a wedge-shaped writing system of communication carved on clay tablets using Phonograms.

1500 BC - Phoenicians

The Phoenicians developed a system comprised of 22 symbols that corresponded to 22 spoken sounds of their language.

800 BC - Greek Civilization

The Greeks added vowels and named the symbols of the Phoenician's invention and developed the boustrophedon writing system.

700 BC - Roman Civilization

The Romans further developed the Greek's writing system by adding more letters, bringing it closer to the modern age alphabet.

114 AD - Trajan's Column

The Trajan's Column, one of the best known and the most elegant and exquisitely balanced examples of the Roman letterforms.

1045 - Movable Type

The movable type, a system of printing and typography that uses movable components to reproduce letters on documents appeared in China.

After 1400 AD
* * *

1448 - Guttenberg's Movable Type

Germany's Johannes Guttenberg is credited with the invention and mass production of movable type.

1450 - Punch Cutting

Francesco Griffo introduced the art of punch cutting, which made the movable type possible and designed the first italic type.

1460 - Nicolas Jenson

Nicolas Jenson is credited with being the creator of early Roman typeface and spreaded it across Europe after opening printery in Venice.

1540 - Claude Garamond

Claude Garamond begins a Humanist type movement. He was a highly regarded French typefounder specializing in type for the publishing industry.

16th Century - Garamond Typeface

Claude Garamond designed the Garamond, a group of many serif typefaces. Garamond's typefaces were used widely in many printings.

1739 - William Caslon

William Caslon founded the Caslon Foundry. His types were popular among clients because of their distinctiveness and beauty.

18th Century - Caslon Typeface

William Caslon created the Caslon typestyles, a typeface that modernized the printed book industry.

Before & After 1850 BC
* * *

1706 - 1775 - Baskerville

John Baskerville created the Baskerville typeface. It is one of the most influential and widely used serif typefaces.

1740 - 1813 - Bodoni

Giambattista Bodini is considered to be the father of the modern typestyle, which is characterized by flat serifs and high contrast between thick and thin strokes.

1850's - Arts & Crafts

The Arts & Crafts movement was initiated in Britain and flourished in Europe and North America for "good design, good society."

1865 - 1947 - Goudy

Frederick W. Goudy designed for many publishing houses and companies, popular typefaces includes Goudy Old Style, Californian, and Bulmer.

1872 - Morris Fuller Benton

Morris Fuller Benton creates 221 prolific typefaces, including revivals of historical models, like Bodoni.

1878 - Futura

Paul Renner designed the Futura typefaces and combined the traditional values with modern type.

1890 - Art Nouveau

The Art Nouveau Movement started to spread, it attempted to bring organic design inspired by nature to life and applied art.

Before & After 1930 BC
* * *

1917 - Bauhaus Modernism

The Bauhaus movement had a profound influence on modern and comtemporary art by combining the fine arts with arts and crafts.

1919 - Constructivism

The Constructivism movement aimed to reflect modern industrial society, affecting multiple disciplines with clean lines and pure shapes.

1930's - Art Deco

Art Deco reflects the style of glamour and luxury featuring rich materials, fine craftsmanship, and symmetrical geometric shapes.

1950's - The Swiss Style

The International Typographic Style movement originated from Switzerland and Germany, it incorporates both type and photography.

1957 - Helvetica

The widely used modern san-serif typeface, Helvetica, was created by Swiss typeface designer Max Miedinger.

1968 - Avant Garde

Herb Lubalin, an American graphic designer, published Avant Garde typefaces, a geometric sans serif font family used in the Avant Gard magazine.

1984 - Emigre

Zuzana Licko founded the digital type foundry, Emigre; she's the creator of numerous digital typefaces, including Mrs Eaves.
