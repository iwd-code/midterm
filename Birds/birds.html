Top 8 Endangered Birds

Kakapo

The kakapo also called owl parrot , is a species of large, flightless, nocturnal, ground-dwelling parrot of the super-family Strigopoidea, endemic to New Zealand. The kakapo is critically endangered; the total known adult population is 209 living individuals, all of which are named and tagged, confined to four small islands off the coast of New Zealand that have been cleared of predators.
(Info from wikipedia/ image from Allie_Caulfield)

Fruit Dove

The fruit doves, also known as fruit pigeons, are a genus of birds in the pigeon and dove family. These colourful, frugivorous doves are found in forests and woodlands in Southeast Asia and Oceania. It is a large genus with over 50 species, some threatened or already extinct.
(Info from wikipedia/ image from DickDaniels)

Snowy Owl

Snowy owls are native to the Arctic regions of both North America and the Palearctic, breeding mostly on the tundra. Most owls sleep during the day and hunt at night, but the snowy owl is often active during the day, especially in the summertime.
(Info from wikipedia/ image photographed by Jongsun Lee)

California Condor

The California condor (Gymnogyps californianus) is a New World vulture, and the largest North American land bird. The species is listed by the IUCN as critically endangered.
(Info from wikipedia/ image from Stacy from San Diego)

African Grey Parrot

The grey parrot is an Old World parrot in the family Psittacidae. Grey parrots may live for 40–60 years in captivity, although their mean lifespan in the wild appears to be shorter — approximately 23 years.
(Info from wikipedia/ image from L.Miguel Bugallo Sánchez)

New Zealand storm petrel

The New Zealand storm petrel (Fregetta maoriana) is a small seabird of the family Oceanitidae. As of 2010, it is ranked on the IUCN Red List as critically endangered.
(Info from wikipedia/ image from Aviceda)

Gunnison Sage-grouse

Gunnison sage-grouse are notable for their elaborate courtship rituals. Each spring, males congregate on leks and perform a "strutting display".
(Info from wikipedia/ image from Carlos H. Pacheco)

Florida Grasshopper Sparrow

These guys should be "Grassrunner" Sparrow. They nest on the ground and have a habit of running through the grass to evade you. The only time they pop up is during nesting season when any elevated perch will suffice.
(Info from wikimedia/ image from Gregory "Slobirdr" Smith)

Facts

Reasons Birds Go Extinct

There are many factors contributing to bird species going extinct, some main reasons includes:

  • Habitat loss through development, natural disasters, climate change, etc.
  • Food loss through competition from other species or loss of food sources.
  • Hunting and poaching, as well as invasive predators and egg collecting.
  • Bird trafficking & destructive human activities around the world.

How to Reduce Bird Extinction Risks

There are steps we can take to help birds survive better and longer on earth.

  1. Be Aware of Endangered Species
  2. Support Bird Conservation Programs
  3. Protect Diverse Bird Habitats
  4. Be a Responsible Birder
  5. Share Your Love of Birding
  6. Curb Artificial Risks of Birds
  7. Live in Balance with the Planet

As of December 2019, the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) lists 225 critically endangered avian species, including 19 which are tagged as possibly extinct or possibly extinct in the wild.

The IUCN Red List of Threatened Species

The IUCN Red List of Threatened Species™ is the world's most comprehensive inventory of the global conservation status of plant and animal species. It uses a set of quantitative criteria to evaluate the extinction risk of thousands of species. These criteria are relevant to most species and all regions of the world. With its strong scientific base, The IUCN Red List is recognized as the most authoritative guide to the status of biological diversity. Specific species of parrots, doves, and owls.
